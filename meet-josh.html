Meet Josh

A real story of resilience, hope, and the need for opportunity.

Josh collecting plastic bottles to support his family.

Josh is a 10-year-old boy from Hoima, Uganda. He is the firstborn in a family of four children raised by hardworking parents who struggle to meet daily needs.


One Wednesday morning, while other children were in school, I found Josh at a dumping site collecting plastic bottles. I asked him why he wasn’t in class.


His answer was simple, but powerful — he has never been to school.


Despite that, Josh told me one word he knows in English: “Doctor.” That is his dream.

Talking with Josh about his life, dreams, and challenges.

I spent time talking with him and learning more about his life. Josh works every day collecting plastic bottles to help support himself and his family.


When I asked him how much he earns, he told me he makes about UGX 150 per kilogram.


That small amount is what stands between him and the opportunity to go to school.

Where Josh sells the plastic bottles he collects.

Josh later showed me where he sells the bottles. After that, we shared a simple lunch together and continued talking.


Before leaving, I gave him a small amount of money and promised to visit him again.


But the truth is — Josh doesn’t need temporary help. He needs a real opportunity to go to school and build his future.
